# CARE principles

While the [FAIR principles](/topics/fair-principles) focuses on machine availability of data, the CARE principles aim at protecting indigenous people's rights to governance indigenous data. In <a href="https://doi.org/10.5334/dsj-2020-043" target="_blank">Carroll, et al (2020)</a> CARE was defined as Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, and Ethics. In <a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41559-023-02161-2" target="_blank">Jennings, et al 2023</a> these principles, and how they can be applied by institutions and researchers within the biodiversity field, are explained. Below, please find an adaptation of this article, principle by principle (click on the buttons for detailed information).

## Collective benefit
Data and metadata should be easy to find by both humans and computer systems. Basic machine readable descriptive metadata allows the discovery of interesting datasets and services.
